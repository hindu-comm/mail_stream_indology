> First, you can read the files directly just on the SARIT website, of
> course.   SARIT turns the XML into HTML for you.
>
> But if you want to download the XML and use it some different way, the
> best and easiest converter I know is OxGarage<http://oxgarage.oucs.ox.ac.uk:8080/ege-webclient/>.
> Select "documents" and then "TEI P5", on the left, and then whatever you
> want on the right (PDF, HTML, MS Word, LaTeX, epub (Kindle), etc.).  It
> does a pretty good job, and is constantly improving.
>
> One of our plans for SARIT is to build this conversion facility right into
> the SARIT website, so you can just press a button right there beside the
> text and take delivery of whatever format you want.  It is likely to be
> handled by OxGarage, which offers a RESTful<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Restful>service (i.e., it can be used in the background by other websites).
>
