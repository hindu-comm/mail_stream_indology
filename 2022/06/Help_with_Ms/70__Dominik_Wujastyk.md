> See for the online description:
> https://archivesetmanuscrits.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/cc96922b
>
> See for the digitised microfilm:
> https://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/btv1b10082949x
>
> This BnF Sanscrit 132 is described by Cabaton 1907 (
> https://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/btv1b10536321f/f32.item), as noted in
> your *Indian System of Human Marks*, and by Jean Filliozat 1941 (
> https://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/bpt6k255707s/f105.item).
>
> From Filliozat’s description we learn that this MS was part of the Hodgson
> collection and was donated to the future BnF in 1840 by the Société
> Asiatique.
>
> It seems thus, to answer your question if I understand it correctly, that
> both shelfmarks (Société Asiatique C5 and Sanscrit 132 in the BnF) could
> designate one and the same manuscript.
>
> One question of interest to me. Where did you find the shelfmark “C5”. I
> guess it is from J. Filliozat, “*Catalogue* des *manuscrits sanskrits**
> et tibétains de la* *Société asiatique*”, JA CCXXXIII, 1941-1942, to
> which I have, unfortunately, no access.
>
> If anybody on the list has a soft copy of this article by Fillozat, I
> would be very grateful for sharing it.
>
