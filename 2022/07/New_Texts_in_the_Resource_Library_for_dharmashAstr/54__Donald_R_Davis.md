Dear Colleagues,

We are pleased to announce that several new electronic texts are now available in the Resource Library for Dharmaśāstra Studies<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/resources/dharmasastra/> at UT-Austin. The usual caveats about typos in these transcriptions apply. We want to share these files as soon as they are complete, but we also try continually to update any errors we find or that come to our attention.


  *   Sarasvatīvilāsa by Pratāparudra<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/sarasvativilasa/>, digest on legal procedure
  *   Pārāśara-mādhavīya<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/resources/dharmasastra/parasara-madhaviya/>, commentary on the Parāśara-smṛti
  *   Divyatattva of Raghunandana Bhaṭṭācārya<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/resources/dharmasastra/divyatattva/>, digest on ordeals and oaths
  *   Haradatta’s commentary (Mitākṣarā) on Gautama-dharmasūtra<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/resources/dharmasastra/haradatta-gautama/>
  *   Haradatta’s commentary (Ujjvalā) on Āpastamba-dharmasūtra<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/resources/dharmasastra/haradatta-apastamba/>
  *   Vyavahāranirṇaya of Varadarāja<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/vyavaharanirnaya/>, digest on legal procedure
  *   Haradatta’s commentary (Anākulā) on Āpastamba-gṛhyasūtra<https://sites.utexas.edu/sanskrit/haradatta-apastamba-grhyasutra/>

Best,

Don Davis
Dept. of Asian Studies
University of Texas at Austin
