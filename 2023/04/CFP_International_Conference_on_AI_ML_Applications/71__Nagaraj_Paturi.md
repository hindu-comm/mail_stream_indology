Call for Papers

International Conference on AI & ML Applications to Computational Sanskrit,
Natural Language Processing, and Cognition-*AIMLSNLP2023*



We invite submissions for the International Conference on AI & ML
Applications to Computational Sanskrit, Natural Language Processing, and
Cognition. *AIMLSNLP 2023 *will be held in BITS Pilani, Hyderabad, India,
from December 22-23, 2023 and in Technical Collaboration with ACM Hyderabad
Deccan. The conference aims to bring together researchers, scholars, and
practitioners in the fields of artificial intelligence, machine learning,
computational linguistics, cognitive science, and related disciplines to
share their latest research, developments, and applications in these areas.

Papers may cover a broad range of topics related to AI & ML applications in
computational Sanskrit, natural language processing, and cognition,
including but not limited to:

   - Natural language processing techniques for Sanskrit text analysis
   - Machine learning algorithms for Sanskrit language understanding and
   translation
   - Computational models of Sanskrit grammar and syntax
   - Knowledge representation and reasoning in Sanskrit computational
   linguistics
   - Semantic analysis of Sanskrit texts using AI & ML techniques
   - Applications of AI & ML in Sanskrit language learning and pedagogy
   - Cognitive modelling of Sanskrit language processing using AI & ML
   approaches
   - Corpus-based studies of Sanskrit texts using AI & ML methods
   - Large Language Models
   - Embedding /vector representation for ML Algorithms
   - Neural Machine Translation(NMT)-State of Art
   - Knowledge Graphs and Structured Prediction
   - Deep Learning techniques for
   - Information Extraction
   - Document Summarization
   - Text Classification
   - Question Answering
   - CNNs for  historical document
   - Image processing
   - Character and word recognition
   - OCR development
   - Agent Mining
   - Multi Agent Systems
   - Representation Learning
   - Dictionary Learning
   - Discriminative Learning
   - Sequential Learning
   - Transfer Learning
   - Generative Learning
   - Adversarial Learning
   - Conversational AI
   - Augmented AI
   - Machines with human like Learning and Thinking
   - Model Building and Cognition

We welcome both theoretical and applied research papers on these topics.













Submission Guidelines:

   - Papers should be original and not previously published or currently
   under review elsewhere.
   - Manuscripts should be written in English and follow the conference's
   formatting guidelines.
   - Manuscripts should be submitted through the online submission system.
   - All manuscripts will be subject to a rigorous peer-review process.



Important Dates:

   - Abstract Submission Deadline: July 9,2023 AOE
   - Full Paper Submission Deadline: September 24, 2023 AOE
   - Notification of Acceptance:  October 28 , 2023
   - Camera-Ready Paper Submission: November 26 , 2023
   - Conference Dates:  December 22,23 , 2023

We look forward to receiving your submissions and having fruitful
discussions on the latest advances in AI & ML applications to computational
Sanskrit, natural language processing, and cognition at the International
Conference.



Policies

Deadlines:

Abstract and paper submission deadlines are strict. In no circumstances
will extensions be given.

Changes of title/abstract/authorship:

Authors should include a full title for their paper, as well as a complete
abstract by the abstract submission deadline. Submission titles should not
be modified after the abstract submission deadline, and abstracts should
not be modified by more than 50% after the abstract submission deadline.
Submissions violating these rules may be deleted after the paper submission
deadline without reviewing. The author list at the abstract submission
deadline will be considered final, and no changes in authorship will be
permitted for accepted papers.

Double-Blind Review:

All submissions must be anonymized and may not contain any information with
the intention or consequence of violating the double-blind reviewing
policy, including (but not limited to) citing previous works of the authors
or sharing links in a way that can infer any author’s identity or
institution, actions that reveal the identities of the authors to potential
reviewers.

Dual Submission:

It is not appropriate to submit papers that are identical (or substantially
similar) to versions that have been previously published, accepted for
publication, or submitted in parallel to other conferences or journals.
Such submissions violate our dual submission policy, and the organizers
have the right to reject such submissions, or to remove them from the
proceedings.

Reviewing Criteria:

Accepted papers must be based on original research and must contain
significant novel results of significant interest to the machine learning
community. Results can be either theoretical or empirical. Results will be
judged on the degree to which they have been objectively established and/or
their potential for scientific and technological impact. Reproducibility of
results and easy availability of code will be taken into account in the
decision-making process whenever appropriate.

Ethics:

Authors and members of the program committee, including reviewers, are
expected to follow standard ethical guidelines. Plagiarism in any form is
strictly forbidden as is unethical use of privileged information by
reviewers, such as sharing it or using it for any other purpose than the
reviewing process. All suspected unethical behaviors will be investigated
by an ethics board and individuals found violating the rules may face
sanctions. Details of the guideline will be published on the website.
