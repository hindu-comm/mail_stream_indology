Dear list members,

I am looking for a manuscript of Ubhayagrastarāhūdaya or Ubhayagrāsarāhūdaya of Vijayīndratīrtha, an allegorical drama on the lines of the Prabodhacandrodaya and Saṅkalpasūryodaya. It is listed in the List of Sanskrit manuscripts in Private Libraries of Southern India by Gustav Oppert (vol. 1, no. 2504, p. 228) as being in the collection of Vīrasvāmī Aiyaṅgār from Sivaganga in Madhurai district (link here: Lists Of Sanskrit Manuscripts In Private Libraries Vol.1 : Oppert, Gustav, Comp. : Free Download, Borrow, and Streaming : Internet Archive<https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.53564/page/n235/mode/2up>).

I would be grateful for any information on where manuscripts from this particular collection may be found—have they been moved to any library, or would anyone have the contact details of whoever has access to the private collection?

Thank you,

Anusha S Rao
PhD Candidate
Department for the Study of Religion
University of Toronto
