I completely agree with the statement of Thomas Burrow that Dominik
reported. A completely unified language without variation is an illusion,
often created by the standardized "official" versions. In Marathi, there is
a proverb that says that language changes after every 12 Kos [Skt. Krośa =
2 miles?]. As Kiparsky brought out vividly, Pāṇini's Sanskrit also has a
great deal of variation, and one can indeed think of various local dialects
or varieties of Sanskrit.

Madhav
