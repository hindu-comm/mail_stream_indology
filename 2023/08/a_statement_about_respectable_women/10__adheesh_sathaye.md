Dear Gaia and list members,

Iin the Bṛhatsaṃhitā of Varāhamihira (78.8), (and reused in the Vetālapañcaviṃśati of Śivadāsa (3.16)), the opposite is given as an indication of a woman who has lost her passion - viraktaceṣṭā:

स्पृष्ट्वाथवालोक्य धुनोति गात्रं 
करोति गर्वं न रुणद्धि यान्तम् । 
चुम्बाविरामे वदनं प्रमार्ष्टि 
पश्चात्समुत्तिष्ठति पूर्वसुप्ता ॥ १६ ॥

a free translation: 

As soon as you touch her, or even look at her, her body shakes in fear, 
She’ll be prideful, and not stop you when you’re leaving. 
She’ll wipe her face after you’re done making out, 
She’ll fall asleep before you, and wake up later. 

hope that might be helpful in your analysis! 

all best wishes,
Adheesh


—
Adheesh Sathaye
University of British Columbia
