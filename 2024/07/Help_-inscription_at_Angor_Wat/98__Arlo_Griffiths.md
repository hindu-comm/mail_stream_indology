With thanks to my colleague CHHOM Kunthea, I can answer as follows:

It is the inscription classified as K. 302 in the EFEO inventory of Cambodian inscriptions or IMA (Inscriptions modernes d'Angkor) 3 in the list proposed by Saveros LEWITZ/POU.

The language is Middle Khmer in the corresponding variety of Khmer script.

See, i.a.,

Lewitz, Saveros. 1970. “Textes en khmer moyen: inscriptions modernes d’Angkor 2 et 3.” Bulletin de l’École française d’Extrême-Orient 57: 99–126. [DOI: 10.3406/befeo.1970.5413<https://doi.org/10.3406/befeo.1970.5413>].

Kunthea will soon encode the inscription so that it will become available at dharmalekha.info.
