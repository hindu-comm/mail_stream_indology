Dear colleagues,

apologies for cross-posting. I would like to draw your attention to the 
latest issue (20/2024) of the Vienna journal "medieval worlds" (Open 
Access), which contains two thematic sections, one on "Moving Jobs: 
Occupational Identity and Motility in the Middle Ages" and the other on 
"Cultural Brokers in European and Asian Contexts". Both sections contain 
articles dealing with issues of relevance to South Asian, Tibetan and 
Buddhist Studies:

Tomas Larsen Høisæter: Forms of Mobility in the Southern Tarim Basin in 
the 7th to 10th Centuries

Kurtis R. Schaeffer: Cultural Brokers and Other Historiographic 
Metaphors: Perspectives from Indo-Tibetan Cultural History

Christina Pecchia: Scholarly Personae in Colonial South Asia: Cultural 
Brokers and their Antagonists

The issue can be accessed at 
https://verlag.oeaw.ac.at/produkt/medieval-worlds-comparative-and-interdisciplinary-studies-no-20-2024/medieval-20-2024?name=medieval-worlds-comparative-and-interdisciplinary-studies-no-20-2024&product_form=5443. 


With best regards,
