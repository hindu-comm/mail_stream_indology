Perhaps one part of this might be for members to identify the significant
unpublished material of others to preserve.

For example:
*Part 2 Verba Indoarica.*
This was posted on the Indology list in 2019 by Asko Parpola:
A little earlier, 14 Nov 2017, he had written me: "For the time being I am
concentrating on the second volume of my *VIA [Verba Indo-Arica]* (II: *Radices
secundariae*) for which I’ll be looking for a fine printer in the second
half of next year.” Although the first volume, I: *Radices primariae *(1997,
600 pp.), was printed by the publishing house of the Viennese Academy of
Sciences, it was not included in any publication series (apparently the
author was too shy to ask), and Werba had to pay the printing himself.  It
is to be hoped that his Viennese colleagues now have the second volume duly
published in an appropriate series.

*Part 2 Avesta Grammar in comparison with Sanskrit by A. V. Williams
Jackson*
I vaguely recall being told or reading somewhere that the manuscript for
this unpublished part 2  is sitting in a university archive somewhere
(Columbia?)
