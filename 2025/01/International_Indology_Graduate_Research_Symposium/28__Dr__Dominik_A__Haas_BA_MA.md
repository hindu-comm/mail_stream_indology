Dear Colleagues,

With many thanks to Lidia Wojtczak, who has taken care of the 
//*International Indology Graduate Research Symposium *(IIGRS) since 
2019, Vitus Angermeier and I are honored to take over responsibility for 
this event format as members of the Steering Committee at the beginning 
of this year. In the course of this there are a few updates.

– After a few short breaks during the pandemic, we are working towards 
holding the IIGRS *every year* again. The last IIGRS was held in 
December last year at Toyo University in Japan, and we already have 
(potential) locations for 2025 and 2026. Keep an eye open for the 
upcoming CfPs.

– The proceedings of symposiums (/Puṣpikā/) are published by the Fair 
Open Access publisher *Heidelberg Asian Studies Publishing* 
(https://hasp.ub.uni-heidelberg.de/catalog/series/puspika). Fair Open 
Access means that the opportunity to publish research results depends 
only on the scientific quality – and not on the financial or 
institutional situation of the editors and authors.

– The *publication guidelines* for Puṣpikā editors have been updated 
(https://iigrs.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/pue1b9a3pika_guidelines.pdf). 


– The *website *has been updated to reflect all these changes 
(https://iigrs.wordpress.com/).

If you have any questions, please write to iigrscontact at gmail.com

Happy 2025 and best regards,
Dominik A. Haas & Vitus Angermeier
