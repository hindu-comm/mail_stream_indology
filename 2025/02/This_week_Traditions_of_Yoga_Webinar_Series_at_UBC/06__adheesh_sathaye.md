Dear Colleagues,

I’m delighted to invite you all to our regular “Traditions of Yoga” webinar series at UBC! We hope you can join us on Zoom this Wednesday, Feb 5, 5-7PM Pacific Time, as we welcome Zoë Slatoff (Loyola Marymount University) to deliver a presentation titled "A Mini-Yoga Retreat: Ancient Technology for Modern Times.” 

TRADITIONS OF YOGA: A WEBINAR SERIES AT UBC
Feb 5, Mar 12, Apr 9, 2025
ZOOM REGISTRATION: https://tinyurl.com/p9avs84f <https://tinyurl.com/p9avs84f>

UBC Asian Studies is excited to partner with Yogic Studies to present Traditions of Yoga, an annual webinar series that brings together UBC students, Vancouver-area yoga professionals and practitioners, as well as interested scholars, students and practitioners worldwide to discuss new ideas, trends, and critical issues in traditional & contemporary yoga.

The series is held annually in conjunction with an undergraduate course at UBC, ASIA 210: Traditions of Yoga. We are pleased to open the doors of our classroom and invite you to join us through Zoom on select Wednesdays this spring as we welcome noted scholars to discuss how and why they study yoga through historical, cultural, and critical lenses.

For more information, including full  talk descriptions and bios, please visit: https://blogs.ubc.ca/yogatraditions <https://blogs.ubc.ca/yogatraditions>

TALK SCHEDULE:

February 5, 5-7PM Pacific Time
Zoë Slatoff, Loyola Marymount University
A Mini-Yoga Retreat: Ancient Technology for Modern Times

March 12, 5-7PM Pacific Time
Varun Khanna, Swarthmore College
Sanskrit and the Importance of Aurality in Yogic Practice

April 9, 5-7PM Pacific Time
Seth Powell, Yogic Studies
Bridging the Studio and the Academy: the Future of Yoga Studies?

All talks are live-streamed on Zoom, and free and open to the public with registration. Recordings will not be available, but we are planning to release a summary video at a later date.

Hope to see you in the chat!
