Dear List,

Here's an opportunity for those who would like to spend 15 days in a European research institution having special collections related to the study of religions (see the list of partners in the attachment or on the sites in the message). For instance, among the partners, I note the interest in the Indological field of:
• the École Pratique des Hautes Études in Paris
• the Bibliothèque nationale et universitaire de Strasbourg
(about which, see:
https://hisaar.unistra.fr/agenda/manuscrits-et-outils-de-travail-des-indianistes-du-xixe-s-conserves-a-la-bnu-de-strasbourg/
https://www.bnu.fr/fr/evenements-culturels/agenda-culturel/manuscrits-et-outils-de-travail-des-indianistes-du-19e-siecle
For a few digitalized mss., see: https://archivesetmanuscrits.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/cc959727
https://www.numistral.fr/fr/tresors/un-manuscrit-jaina-sur-papier-indien-du-15e-siecle )
and in the "IT[alian]SERR consortium", the libraries of
• the University of Naples “l’Orientale”
• the University of Turin (Eastern Library “Oscar Botto”)
(see https://www.resilience-ri.eu/our-collections-9/ )

Accommodation and travel costs are of the responsibility of the candidates, but they will enjoy a warm welcome and on-site support.

Fifth Call for Applications: Transnational Access Fellowships 2025-2026

RESILIENCE, the European cross-disciplinary research infrastructure serving the study of religion, launches its fifth call for applications for Transnational Access Fellowships. The call will be open March 13 to May 1, 2025. Users will gain direct, fast, and effective access to the collections of leading research institutions and universities in Europe, guided by experts in the field. As more and more institutions are joining RESILIENCE as TNA hosts, the range of offerings has expanded considerably.

More can be read in the attached press release, which is also online accessible<https://www.resilience-ri.eu/news/fifth-call-for-applications-transnational-access-fellowships-2025-2026/>.

Thank you very much for your attention. Sharing the press release is appreciated.
