Dear McComas Taylor,

According to uṇādi-sūtra ‘divo dve dīrghaś cābhyāsakhyaḥ’ (Siddhāntakaumudīno. 495/Ujjvaladatta 4.55), the word dīdivi is derived from the root div (class4) with the affix KviN. In his Bālamanoramā under this sūtra, Vāsudeva Dīkṣita quotesṛgveda 1.1.8 “gopām ṛtasya dīdivim” and says that the word is used with meaningof ‘dyotamāna’.

Yours
Sharon
