Dear Friends,

Next week, we are organizing two info sessions about our MA programme in South
Asian Studies <https://www.india.ugent.be/english/>. If you have promising
BA students who are looking to begin graduate work in any of our expertises
and specialisation options (Sanskrit, Prakrit, Buddhism, Jainism, Islam in
South Asia, textuality, material culture, art history, etc.), please send
this information their way.

We are holding two info sessions, one suitable for North American time
zones and one suitable for time zones in Asia. Folk can register to either
to receive a meeting link:

On Tuesday March 18th, at 17:00 CET (IST 21:30; EDT 12:00):
https://event.ugent.be/registration/InfosessionMA1
<https://event.ugent.be/registration/InfosessionMA1?fbclid=IwZXh0bgNhZW0CMTAAAR0Q53d5SsPsQCU92WMvJT_L42SHFJYkZPkS7tBz3czVJlth3nE8ToxrvRY_aem_G67ZBjcHbj9gdSNyxMU92Q>

On Wednesday March 19th, at 11:00 CET (IST 16:00)
https://event.ugent.be/registration/infosessionMA2
<https://event.ugent.be/registration/infosessionMA2?fbclid=IwZXh0bgNhZW0CMTAAAR06CBb2sp7ltKumLmmK6qrty8gmChsBdbBKree-6e-lkngSRQSMORE2FEM_aem_hehy2WJukPvN0G6GuSqg8Q>

Perhaps this may be of special interest to young folk in North America who
may be eager, for whatever reason, to see what life is like on another
continent. However, for those who do not wish to leave the confines of
their current location we also offer the option to follow the Master
programme in South Asian Studies at Ghent University completely long
distance. Our classes are streamed live and students from anywhere in the
world can join us. The degree is the same as the regular MA in South Asian
Studies. Isn't that convenient?

With my best wishes,
Charles
