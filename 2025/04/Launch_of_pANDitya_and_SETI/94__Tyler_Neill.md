Dear List Members,

I'd like to share a pair of projects I've been working on for the past few
months, both aimed at greater understanding of Sanskrit works and their
authors, especially through increased accessibility to e-texts.

1. *Pāṇḍitya* is an online graph visualization tool illustrating
connections between works and authors in the Pandit database. It is live at
panditya.info, and I plan to present a paper on it this summer at WSC 19.
Blog post: Pāṇḍitya: A New Visual Exploration Tool for Sanskrit
Intellectual Networks
<https://www.tylerneill.info/blog-kalpataru-diaries/panditya>.

2. *SETI*, or the *Sanskrit E-Text Inventory*, is a metadata aggregation
project that both stands alone as an open spreadsheet and integrates with
Pāṇḍitya to allow navigating to online e-texts from its graphical
interface. More info at panditya.info/seti.
Blog post: SETI: The New Register for Sanskrit E-Texts
<https://www.tylerneill.info/blog-kalpataru-diaries/seti>.

May these resources be useful to you and your students, and please be in
touch with feedback!

Kind regards,
Tyler
