apologies for cross-posting.

this is to inform you of a new publication in the series "Sanskrit Texts 
from the Tibetan Autonomous Region" published jointly by the Austrian 
Academy of Sciences Press in Vienna and the China Tibetology Publishing 
House in Beijing:

Tattvārthā. Sthiramati’s Abhidharmakośaṭīkā, Chapter 1, Part I. 
Diplomatically edited by Nobuchiyo Odani in collaboration with Kazunobu 
Matsuda and Hironori Tanaka.

Based on a newly discovered Sanskrit manuscript from Tibet, the book 
contains the first part of the diplomatic edition of Sthiramati's 
commentary on Vasubandhu's Abhidharmakośabhāṣya, which is more 
ideological, philosophical, and challenging to comprehend compared to 
Yaśomitra's more widely known Sphuṭārthā and complements the latter's 
remarks, which focus on terminology and grammatical explanation. The 
Tattvārthā is a crucial resource for tracing the controversy between the 
Sarvāstivāda and the Sautrāntika in Sanskrit and for elucidating the 
profound differences in thought between these two schools.
