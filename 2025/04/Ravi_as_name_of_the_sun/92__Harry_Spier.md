Thank you to both Lubomir Ondracka and to Madhav Deshpande (offline)  who
both pointed me to Mayrhofer.

1) Note that both EWA and KEWA are downloadable from archive.org
https://archive.org/details/mayrhofer-dicts/KEWA-Bd1-%28A-Tha%291956/

2) I was struck at how different the entry for "ravi" was in KEWA from that
in EWA. I then found this interesting  article by Paul Thieme 'On M.
Mayrhofer's "Etymologisches Wörterbuch des Altindoarischen"' in JSTOR
  https://www.jstor.org/stable/620576
where he notes at the beginning of the article ". . .EWA ...is not , as the
author rightly insists a new and improved edition of KEWA, but a renewed
attempt in its own rights. . ."

Thanks,

> (vgl. ai. aru-ṇá-, aru-ṣá- [anders o. I 49], bzw. *reu-dh- s. rudhiráḥ),
