Dear Harry,

If it may be of interest to you, I just published a book on list-making in the context of Indian philosophy, in gnostic yogas more precisely. The first part is fully dedicated to theorizing list-making practices and taxonomy. I do not so much engage with listing names of deities however, but could refer you off-list to a literature that does this in other world traditions.

See: Metaphysics as Therapy: List-Making and Renunciation in Gnostic Yogas | SpringerLink<https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-981-96-2556-7>
[https://media.springernature.com/w153/springer-static/cover/book/978-981-96-2556-7.jpg]<https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-981-96-2556-7>
Metaphysics as Therapy - Springer<https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-981-96-2556-7>
This book examines the significance of metaphysical list-making as a determining feature of spiritual exercises in South Asian gnostic yogas
