> 2*.**    Why should you not eat after sunset ? *
>
>    - In Jainism, not eating after sunset is a significant practice rooted
>       in the principle of Ahimsa (non-violence) and practical considerations.
>       Here's why:
>       - Protection of Microscopic Life: "Small insects cannot be seen at
>       night, therefore one should not eat sweet balls even; even the omniscient
>       have not done that." The primary reason is to avoid unintentionally harming
>       minute organisms that become active and visible in the dark.
>       - Visibility: "The germs and worms etc., falling into the dinner
>       are not seen by the people as their eyes are blinded by the dense darkness.
>       Therefore, who would eat at night?".
>       - Scriptural Basis: "One should not eat after sunset as the food is
>       polluted by the goblins who are moving uncontrollably at night".
>       - Health and Digestion: "Taking meals before sunset and after
>       sunrise has been an identification mark of Jains traditionally. This is not
>       only to avoid violence to insects and other small organisms, which are not
>       clearly visible at night but also for better health. Medical science
>       endorses this traditional practice on the scientific basis that there
>       should be at least a four hours gap between meals and sleep for better
>       digestion By restricting intake of food before sunset this gap is
>       automatically maintained".
>       - Tradition: "One, who knows religion should never eat after the
>       day is over. Those who are non-Jains also declare that the meal at night is
>       not worth taking".
>       - Closing of Lotuses in the Body: "There are two lotuses in the
>       body, which are closed at sunset. As such, taking food at night amounts to
>       devouring the minute germs; Ayurved (Indian medical science) also specifies
>       that it can lead to major diseases".
>       - Commitment to the Vow: "Even today, there is a section of the
>       Jain population including Jain sadhus, sadhvis and laymen/women who follow
>       this practice and it is certainly worth emulating... Eating food after
>       sunset is a gateway to hell. One should neither eat nor offer any
>       food/drink after sunset".

> *3.    Explain all the Anuyogas in simple words*
>
>    - The Jain Agamas are classified into four Anuyogas, each offering a
>       unique lens through which to understand the teachings. According to Ācārya
>       Ārya Rakṣit, these are:
>       - Dharmakathanuyoga (Narrative Literature): This Anuyoga includes
>       religious tales and narratives.
>       - Charananuyoga (Conduct and Practice): It deals with conduct,
>       behavior, and practical aspects of religious life.
>       - Ganitanuyoga (Mathematics and Cosmology): This covers
>       mathematics, astrology, geography, and cosmology.
>       - Dravyanuyoga (Metaphysics): It concerns itself with entities,
>       both living and nonliving, and metaphysics.
>       - The term "Anuyoga" means "to associate (yoga) the intended and
>       appropriate meaning (anu) with a word".
