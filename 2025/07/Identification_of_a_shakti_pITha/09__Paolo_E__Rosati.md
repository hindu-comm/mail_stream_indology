Dear Indolgists,

In the  *Kulacūḍāmaṇitantra* (3.62) according to L.M. Finn trans.(1986) and
to the Girīsha Chandra Vedāntatīrtha ed. (1915)

At Ṭhehāra  Kāmarūpā and Dikkaravāsinī are to be worshipped (Finn trans.)
Skt.: *kāmarūpāntu ṭhehāre pūjyā dikkaravāsinī*

I am curious to know if anybody found in any text the *Ṭhehāra pitha*,
because I am unable to locate it, although it should be an Assamese site.
It does not appear in Sircar (1948) work on pīṭhas and I never found it
during my studies on  Kāmākhyā. The fact that there is also worshipped
Dikkaravāsinī let me to suppose that it is an eastern assamese site
(cf.  Shastri
ed. and trans. of* Kālikāpurāṇa* 80.36ff.)

Best wishes,
Paolo
