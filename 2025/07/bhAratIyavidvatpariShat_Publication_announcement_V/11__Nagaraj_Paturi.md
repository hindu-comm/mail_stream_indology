Congratulations and thanks for sharing, Prof. Jan E M Houben.

There is an article by Dipak Bhattacharya ji who is a senior member of BVP
also included in this Anthology.

Personally I found the title

On the authenticity of the puberty rituals for girls in the Indian .......49

ritual texts

*Nirmala Kulkarni*

particularly interesting.

Puberty rituals , puberty songs etc., have been studied as part of folklore
studies in regional Indian languages, publications done in those regional
Indian languages.

But this article seems to help trace the Vedic ritual connections helping
my area of focus , the study of folk-Vedic interface.

Your own article on the structure of Vedic ritual , particularly, the
'self-reference ' aspect connects to the other studies of
self-referentiality in Vedic tradition in other areas than ritual.

Overall, all the titles inspire us to go to the book.
