Dear colleagues,

The Pali Text Society is pleased to announce the publication of volume 36 of the Journal of the Pali Text Society. This volume is published online on the Heidelberg Asian Studies Publishing server as an open access journal:

https://hasp.ub.uni-heidelberg.de/journals/jpts/issue/view/1659 <https://hasp.ub.uni-heidelberg.de/journals/jpts/index>

Contents of volume 36
Sodo Mori 森祖道 (1934–2025) (Norihisa Baba)
For the Sake of the Prosperity and Splendour of the Most Excellent Teaching’: The Burmese Saṃgharāja Ñāṇābhivaṃsa’s Letter to the Monastic Community of Lanka (Jens W. Borgland)
Qualities of Distinction: A New Perspective on the dhutaṅgas in the Pāli Canon (Oliver Freiberger)
Conceptualising the World in Pali Literature (Alastair Gornall)
An Early Religious and Historical Tradition of Laos: The Aḍḍhabhāgabuddharūpanidāna of Ariyavaṃsa (Javier Schnake)
Storytelling in the Pāli Nikāyas: the Particle kho and the Textual Cycle of the Sick Monk (Eviatar Shulman)
Pali muṭṭha, mussati, mosa, and Related Words (Martin Straube)
A Note on the Title and Date of Dharmananda Kosambi’s Navanītaṭīkā (Truptirani B. Tayade and Mahesh A. Deokar)


Hard copies of JPTS volume 36 will be available for purchase on the PTS website in September.
