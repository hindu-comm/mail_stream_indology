Dear Colleagues,

I hope you're all doing well!

I would like to draw your attention to an upcoming event hosted by Global
Antiquities at Northwestern University
<https://sites.northwestern.edu/globalantiquitiesnu/>. The second "Teaching
Antiquity Symposium"
<https://sites.northwestern.edu/globalantiquitiesnu/teaching-antiquity-symposium-2026/>will
take place on April 18th, 2026, at Northwestern University on the Evanston
campus.

The event aims to bring together instructors and curators to share ideas
and discuss contemporary developments in the teaching of ancient cultures,
including ancient India.


*The symposium is currently inviting the submission of abstracts for papers
which should be submitted to me (yannicklambert2028 at u.northwestern.edu
<yannicklambert2028 at u.northwestern.edu>).*
*All scholars with a college or museum affiliation* are welcome to submit,
and* graduate student submissions* *are also encouraged.*

For complete details on the submission process, abstract requirements, and
the full range of topics, please* see the attached Call for Papers (CFP).*

Topics are varied and include teaching ancient languages, teaching beyond
the Mediterranean, and digital approaches to antiquity, among others.


*The submission deadline is December 1, 2025.*
The symposium will also feature a keynote address by Professor Clara
Bosak-Shroeder of the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.

Please feel free to share as widely as you like in your departments.

Please let me know if you have any questions.

Many thanks,

Yannick Lambert
