I am pretty sure the passage refers to Suśrutasaṃhitā 1.15.44, in a 
general chapter on the humours/morbific factors (doṣa), bodily 
constituents (dhātu) and waste products (mala):

/samadoṣaḥsamāgniś casamadhātumalakriyaḥ| 
prasannātmendriyamanāḥsvasthāity abhidhīyate/||

Having balanced humours, a balanced digestive fire, balanced activity of 
bodily constituents and waste products, and clear self, senses and mind 
– this is called the natural state.

Best wishes,
Vitus
