Dear list members,
I've been asked to provide a manuscript image of the ādigayatrī mantra from
the ṛgveda that shows the accents. I can find images of accented Rgvedas
online but they all have the accents anudatta and svarita. None I could
find had the double svarita accent. All svarita accents were marked with
the single svarita accent mark , a single vertical line.

But if you look at this video comparing the Shringeri, Kanchi and Varanasi
styles of reciting the ādigayatrī mantra,
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mQ2nYFnMiQk
At 1:37 it shows that the  ādigayatrī  mantra is pronounced with some
double svarita accents.
See below:
[image: image.png]

1) Do ṛgveda manuscripts (or any vedic manuscripts) exist that have the
double svarita marked.  I had assumed this was the case because double
svarita is in the  unicode vedic extensions block.

2) Do ṛgveda manuscripts in scripts other than devanagari mark the accent.
