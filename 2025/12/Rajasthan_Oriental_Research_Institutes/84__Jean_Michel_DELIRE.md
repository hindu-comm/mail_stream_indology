Dear Friends,

I thank you for your help, Tim, Dhaval, Dominik, Shihong Zhao, Elliot and
Christophe.
I have sent a message to the address directorrori at yahoo.in, but received no
answer yet.
I also consulted the page
https://jkk.artandculture.rajasthan.gov.in/content/ArtandCulture/en/rajasthan-oriental-research-institute/facilities-scholars.html,
where I read the following - and rather worrying - phrase : Scholars coming
from abroad will also be entertained, if they are affiliated with any
Indian University, after submission of their credentials from the concerned
embassy.
This seems especially difficult to reach. On the other hand, the same page
also specifies :
Research facilities are available at the Institutes, headquarter and all
the seven branch repositories, for which scholars may contact
1. Director, Rajasthan Oriental Research Institute, P.W.D. Road, Jodhpur.
Telephone No. 2430244
2. Assistant Director, Rajasthan Oriental Research Institute, Inside
Maharaja Sanskrit College, Opposite Old Assembly Hall, S.D. Bazar, Jaipur.
3. Senior Research Assistant, Rajasthan Oriental Research Institute, Mahal
Chowk, Alwar.
4. Senior Research Assistant, Rajasthan Oriental Research Institute, Braja
Vilas Palace, Kota.
5. Senior Research Assistant, Rajasthan Oriental Research Institute, Near
R.D. Girls College, Bharatpur.
6. Senior Research Assistant, Rajasthan Oriental Research Institute, Near
Govt. Press, Opp. Station, Bikaner.
As I am especially interested in manuscripts from Jaipur, Alwar, Kota and
Bikaner, this is encouraging. But, while a telephone number is given
for Jodhpur (for which Dhaval Patel gave me a more recent +91 94612 92170),
there are no tel or Email addresses for the other branches. I wonder if it
is possible to find any more up to date and useful information.
I am also trying to contact the IGNCA and to see what manuscripts this
centre could help me to see and copy. The problem is that their information
seems rather outdated and scanty, at least on the page
https://www.namami.gov.in/major-manuscript-repositories-india.

Best regards,

Jean Michel Delire

>> I think it is a good idea to write to the Jodhpur HQ in any case, out of
>> courtesy.  If they answer, so much the better.  My experiences with branch
>> offices (last time in about 2005) is that they really did want to see
>> *something* official.  A letter from Jodhpur is best.  But also, be armed
>> at least with a letter from your university or institution, as
>> official-looking as possible.  University stamps are always good.
>>
>> IGNCA in Delhi has filmed many RORI manuscripts.  These RORI images are
>> an exception to the general rule that the IGNCA will not give copies to
>> scholars without a letter of permission from the host library.  For RORI,
>> there is a RORI - IGNCA agreement that IGNCA may give scholars images of
>> the RORI manuscripts they have filmed.  So you could start with IGNCA,
>> since they generally answer communications.  Did they film the Euclid MSS?
>> There used to be an online catalogue of MSS at IGNCA (or NAMAMI) but I
>> think it has been withdrawn.
