In Nāgarī manuscripts, copyists mark off the curved upper part of the incorrect vowel with two marks and add a short mark at the bottom of the vertical stroke. See the third line of the attached image. My guess is that the correction practice in Newari manuscripts is similar. The dot may be an accidental mark (a drop of ink).

You can review images of three Newari script manuscripts at the OCHS Manuscript Database Project (https://ochs-database.netlify.app/) to look for corrections of I to ī or ī to i.
